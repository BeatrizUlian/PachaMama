1. When is the best time to visit Chapada of Veadeiros?

The region has a dry season (from April to September) and the rainy season (from October to March). There are tours that are better during the flood season and others in the dry season. For example, the Canyons, the Couros Falls, the Segredo Waterfall among others, are impassable in the rain, while Loquinhas and the Anjos and Arcanjos Waterfalls are too thin in the drought. There is an appropriate itinerary for each season.

2. How far is the campsite from the attractions?

Camping PachaMama is between the city of Alto Paradise and the Village of São Jorge. 17km and 19km respectively. On the highway that leads to the Park Nacional, at the foot of Morro da Baleia, 4 km from Maitreya's Garden. 9 km from Almécegas and São Bento Waterfalls; 14 km from Vale da Lua; 20 km from the National Park; 22 km from Raizama; 23 km from Morada do Sol; 24 km from Loquinhas; 26 km from Cristal.

3. What to take camping?

1. Firewood for fires, to avoid deforestation of the land;

2. Swimming/snorkeling goggles as some waterfalls are more beautiful from below;

3. Warm clothes and blankets, as it is colder at night;

4. Sunscreen;

5. Repellent;

6. Sunglasses, cap or hat for the trails (especially in the National Park);

7. Pillow;

8. Flashlight;

9. Earplugs, as it is common for other people to snore nearby;

10. Cash, as access to the bank is limited and not everywhere accepts cards;

11. A ground cloth to clean the tent before storing;

12. Personal plates, cutlery, glasses and tea towels, as a preventive measure against COVID-19.

13. Your tent, mattress or sleeping bag and personal hygiene items.

4. How is the reservation made?

Reservations are made exclusively using the RESERVE link on this website. An advance deposit of 100% of the amount is required.

5. Is it safe to take children and the elderly?

Yes. The campsite is all grassy, ​​with a safe space for children. For the elderly, it is also an easily accessible place. The child price is for ages zero to 12. Due to the greater need for structure and care for young children and babies, we include them in child values. In the pool, children must be accompanied by an adult. There are some very suitable waterfalls to take them to.

6. Is it possible to visit Chapada dos Veadeiros without a car?

RNo. Those who do not have their own car will depend on guides with cars or rides on the highway. There is no public transport to the attractions. Chapada dos Veadeiros involves 5 municipalities. The main attractions are all kilometers away. Most of the waterfalls are in Alto Paraíso (Vila de São Jorge) and Cavalcante. São Jorge is 36 km from Alto Paraíso and Cavalcante is 91 km away. Even in the waterfalls that are just a few kilometers from the city or town, there is no place for pedestrians on the roads. The only road transport company to Alto Paraíso is Real Expresso - Phone: (61) 3234-8774

7. Is there a covered space for tents?

There is a 40m² roof with a light point (sockets and lamps) with capacity for 6 to 7 tents and we have a covered kiosk also available for tents. There is no additional price to use the location or reservations. It is first come, first served, however, they rarely occupy the entire space.

8. What is the camping reservation cancellation policy?

If you cancel up to 2 days in advance (that is, until 9 am two days before the reservation date), 80% of the amount deposited will be refunded. After the deadline (less than 1 day before booking or after check-in time), the amount deposited will no longer be refunded. This policy is necessary as our places fill up far in advance and we deny hundreds of reservations due to this. When a person quits at the last minute, it is no longer possible to replace that vacancy and not only do we lose the daily rate during a period of high demand, we also lose the opportunity for more people to get to know our business and gain more customer loyalty.

9. Does the Camping accept MotorHomes?

Yes. There is a tap and power plug available.

10. Do you allow pets at Camping PachaMama and Waterfalls?

We do not accept pets at the campsite and the waterfalls also do not allow pets, regardless of size..
